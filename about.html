Program Objectives

The 21A program aims to:

  • Train experts in artificial intelligence capable of understanding, designing, and developing intelligent systems to solve complex problems in various sectors such as finance, industry, and public services.
  • Provide a deep understanding of the theoretical and practical principles underlying artificial intelligence, including advanced techniques in statistics, optimization, machine learning, and deep learning.
  • Develop transversal skills such as interdisciplinary collaboration, effective communication, project management, and critical thinking.
  • Promote innovation and entrepreneurship through AI.

Access Conditions

Admission to the 21A program is based on:

  • Success in the National Common Competition (CNC) for engineering schools and equivalent institutions in Morocco.
  • Specific competition for holders of DEUG SM or SMI, DUT, or equivalent diplomas in computer science-related fields.
  • Two years of preparatory classes for the INP engineering cycle.
  • Study of the dossier for candidates with a License SMA or SMI, Bachelor, LST, or equivalent diploma in applied mathematics or computer science.

Major Competencies

Students will acquire the following competencies:

  • Handling various data sources: collection, efficient storage, and preprocessing of data (structured data, 2D/3D images, texts, audios, videos, IoT data, sensor network data, etc.).
  • Selection of effective machine learning models.
  • Validation and evaluation of models.
  • Interpretation of modeling results.
  • Improvement of model generalization performance.
  • Efficient data processing using distributed and parallel methods.
  • Deployment of AI models in web/mobile applications, embedded systems, sensor networks.
  • Exploration of AI in robotics and multi-agent systems.
  • Interpretability and ethics of learning models.
  • Securing learning models.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of the 21A program are highly sought after in various industries, including:

  • Banking Sector: Financial analysis, fraud detection, and algorithmic trading.
  • Industry: Automation, predictive maintenance, and supply chain optimization.
  • Healthcare: Medical imaging, diagnostics, and personalized medicine.
  • Agriculture: Precision farming, crop monitoring, and yield prediction.
  • Security: Cybersecurity, surveillance, and threat detection.
  • Meteorology: Weather prediction and climate modeling.

Program Modules

The 21A program is structured into six semesters, covering a wide range of topics:

Semester 1

  • Elements of Analysis and Optimization
  • Statistics and Applied Probability
  • Algorithmics and Data Structures
  • Mathematical Modeling and Programming
  • Computer Architecture
  • Power Skills Development
  • Language and Communication 1

Semester 2

  • Databases
  • Networks and Systems
  • Introduction to Artificial Intelligence
  • Web Development Project
  • Object-Oriented Programming
  • Digital Economy
  • Language and Communication 2

Semester 3

  • Machine Learning
  • Statistics and Data Analysis
  • Multimedia Data Processing
  • Distributed Systems and Parallel Computing
  • Embedded Systems and IoT
  • Innovation and Entrepreneurship
  • Language and Communication 3

Semester 4

  • Probabilistic Machine Learning
  • Kernel-Based Learning
  • Artificial Neural Networks and Deep Learning
  • Adaptive and Online Machine Learning
  • Swarm Robotics
  • Machine Learning Operations (MLOps)
  • Language and Communication 4

Semester 5

  • Adversarial Machine Learning
  • Statistical Learning
  • AI and Applications
  • Deep Reinforcement Learning
  • Unstructured Data and Big Data Modeling
  • Soft Skills Development
  • Language and Communication 5

Semester 6

  • Final Project (PFE)
